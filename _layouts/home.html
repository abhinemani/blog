I'm a fan of technology, government, and interesting ideas.

I have most recently served as the City of Los Angeles' first Chief Data Officer, where I learned how data and technology can improve the lives of the residents of our second largest city.

Before, I helped launch, build, and run a non-profit called Code for America, whose interesting idea is that technology can change government. I've also helped organizations including GovDelivery, OpenGov Foundation, and Significance Labs grow and scale.

Beyond that, I've been thinking about things, ranging from political philosophy and cities to data-driven policy and civic tech and startups, and I have gotten the opportunity to travel to cities across the country and around the world to learn what public life in the 21st Century looks like — which makes me excited about what's ahead.

I try (my best) to write things down.

When I do, I'll post them here, along with the standard stuff: bio, talks, press mentions, etc.

For anything else, just ask: @abhinemani or abhi.nemani@gmail.com.
